Tree work in Johnson County falls into two categories: routine maintenance and emergency removal. Routine maintenance (trimming, pruning, shaping) keeps your trees healthy and your property looking good. Emergency removal is what you need after a storm drops a 60-foot oak on your fence line at 11 PM on a Friday.

Either way, you want a tree service that knows what they are doing. Bad pruning can kill a tree. Bad removal can drop a trunk through your roof. We evaluated tree service companies listed on JoCo Home Pros based on ratings, review counts, and services to find the ones worth calling.

## What to Know Before Hiring a Tree Service

**Kansas does not require tree service companies to hold a license.** This means anyone with a chainsaw and a truck can call themselves a tree service. Look for companies with ISA Certified Arborists on staff, proper insurance (general liability and workers comp), and real reviews from real customers.

**Insurance is non-negotiable.** Tree work is dangerous. If a crew member gets hurt on your property and the company does not have workers comp, you could be liable. Ask for a certificate of insurance before they start. Call the insurance company to verify it is active.

**Get multiple quotes for tree removal.** Prices for removing the same tree can vary by 50% or more between companies. Get at least three quotes. The cheapest is not always the best, but neither is the most expensive.

## When to Trim vs. Remove a Tree

| Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Overgrown branches near house or power lines | Trim. No reason to remove a healthy tree. |
| Tree is leaning after a storm | Get an arborist assessment. May need removal or cabling. |
| Tree is dead or dying | Remove. Dead trees drop branches and eventually fall. |
| Tree is healthy but too close to foundation | Remove. Roots can damage foundations, sewer lines, and sidewalks. |
| Tree has broken limbs but is otherwise healthy | Trim the broken limbs. No need to remove the whole tree. |
| Tree has disease or pest infestation | Consult an arborist. Many diseases are treatable. |
| Tree is blocking a view you want | Trim. Removing a healthy tree for a view is possible but irreversible. |

## Red Flags When Hiring a Tree Service

- **They knock on your door uninvited** offering to trim your trees. Legitimate tree services do not go door to door.
- **They ask for payment upfront.** Standard practice is payment upon completion. A deposit for large jobs is reasonable; full payment before starting is not.
- **They cannot provide proof of insurance.** Walk away.
- **Their truck has no company name or phone number.** Fly-by-night operations do not want to be traced.
- **They offer to "top" your tree.** Tree topping (cutting the top off) is harmful to the tree and makes it more dangerous over time. A qualified arborist will never recommend topping.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

1. **Are you insured?** Ask for a certificate of insurance. Call to verify it is active.
2. **Do you have a certified arborist on staff?** ISA certification means they know tree biology, not just chainsaw operation.
3. **Will you provide a written estimate?** Get it in writing before they start.
4. **How will you protect my property?** They should use mats to protect your lawn and avoid dropping branches on fences, cars, or structures.
5. **Do you haul away all debris?** Most do, but confirm. You do not want a pile of branches in your yard.
