## What to Look for in an HVAC Company

Before we get to the list, here is what actually matters when you are picking an HVAC company in Overland Park:

**Response time.** Kansas summers hit 100 degrees and winters drop below zero. If a company cannot get to you within 24 hours for an emergency, keep looking.

**Licensed and insured in Kansas.** Ask for their license number. Johnson County requires HVAC contractors to hold a valid license. If they hem and haw about it, move on.

**Written estimates.** Get at least two quotes for any job over $500. A good HVAC company will give you a written estimate before starting work, not a verbal ballpark.

**Warranty on parts and labor.** The standard is 1 year on labor and 5-10 years on parts. If someone offers less, ask why.

## When to Repair vs. Replace

The general rule: if your system is under 10 years old and the repair costs less than $500, repair it. If it is over 15 years old and the repair is more than a third of replacement cost, replace it.

Here is a quick breakdown:

| System Age | Repair Cost | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Under 10 years | Under $500 | Repair |
| Under 10 years | Over $500 | Get a second opinion before deciding |
| 10-15 years | Under $300 | Repair, but start budgeting for replacement |
| 10-15 years | Over $500 | Replace. You will save on efficiency. |
| Over 15 years | Any | Replace. Repairs will keep coming. |

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

Before you sign anything, ask these five questions:

1. **Are you licensed in Johnson County?** Get the license number and verify it.
2. **Do you provide a written estimate?** Verbal estimates are not worth the paper they are not written on.
3. **What is your warranty on parts and labor?** Standard is 1 year labor, 5-10 years parts.
4. **Can I see proof of insurance?** If a worker gets hurt on your property, you want their insurance covering it, not your homeowners policy.
5. **Do you offer financing?** System replacements are expensive. Many companies offer 0% financing for 12-60 months.
