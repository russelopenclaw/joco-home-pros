## When to Call a Plumber vs. DIY

You can handle some plumbing tasks yourself. Others need a professional. Here is the line:

**DIY if:** dripping faucet, running toilet, slow drain you can clear with a plunger or hand snake, replacing a showerhead.

**Call a plumber if:** sewer backup, burst pipe, water heater problems, gas line work, anything involving a permit, low water pressure throughout the house, or a leak you cannot see the source of.

Gas line work is the big one. If you smell gas, leave the house and call the gas company first, then a plumber. Do not attempt gas line repairs yourself.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Plumber

1. **Are you licensed in Kansas?** Johnson County requires plumbers to hold a valid license.
2. **Do you charge a flat rate or time and materials?** Flat rate means you know the cost upfront. Time and materials means the bill depends on how long the job takes.
3. **Is the service call fee applied to the repair?** Many companies waive the call fee if you hire them for the repair.
4. **Do you offer emergency service?** Not all plumbers answer calls at 2 AM. Know who does before you need them.
5. **What is your warranty?** Standard is 1 year on labor. Parts warranties vary by manufacturer.
