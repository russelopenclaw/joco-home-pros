## What to Know Before Hiring an Electrician

**Kansas requires electrical licenses.** Anyone doing electrical work in Johnson County needs to hold a valid electrician's license. Do not hire an unlicensed handyman for electrical work. If something goes wrong, your homeowners insurance may not cover damage caused by unlicensed work.

**Permits are required for most electrical work.** Any job that involves adding circuits, upgrading your panel, or modifying wiring requires a permit. A good electrician will pull the permit for you. A bad one will skip it and hope the inspector does not notice.

**Get multiple quotes for big jobs.** Panel upgrades, whole-house rewiring, and generator installations can vary by thousands of dollars between companies. Get at least two quotes.

## When You Need an Electrician (and When You Do Not)

**Call an electrician if:**
- Breaker keeps tripping
- Lights flicker when you plug something in
- Outlet feels warm or looks discolored
- You smell burning plastic near an outlet or switch
- You need a new circuit, outlet, or fixture installed
- Your panel is 100 amps and you are adding an EV charger or hot tub

**You can handle yourself if:**
- Swapping a light switch cover (the plastic plate, not the switch itself)
- Replacing a light bulb
- Testing an outlet with a $10 outlet tester

**Call 911 or the fire department if:**
- You see sparks coming from an outlet or panel
- You smell burning and cannot find the source
- Your panel is buzzing loudly or feels hot to the touch

## Questions to Ask Your Electrician

1. **Are you licensed in Kansas?** Get the license number. Verify it.
2. **Will you pull a permit?** For any work beyond replacing a fixture, the answer should be yes.
3. **Do you provide a written estimate?** Verbal estimates are a red flag.
4. **What is your warranty on labor?** Standard is 1 year. Some offer 2-5 years.
5. **Can you provide references from recent jobs?** A good electrician has references ready.
