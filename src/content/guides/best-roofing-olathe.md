## What to Know Before Hiring a Roofer in Olathe

**Storm chasing is a real problem.** After every hail storm, out-of-state roofing companies show up in Johnson County neighborhoods offering "free inspections" and pushing you to file an insurance claim before they even get on the ladder. Some of these companies are fine. Many are not. They take your insurance check, do a mediocre job, and you never hear from them again when the roof leaks.

**How to spot a storm chaser:** They knock on your door uninvited. Their truck has out-of-state plates. They pressure you to sign a contract on the spot. They cannot provide local references. They ask you to sign over your insurance check before starting work.

**What you want instead:** A local company with an established presence in Johnson County, real reviews from real customers, and a physical office you can visit.

## When to Repair vs. Replace Your Roof

| Roof Age | Damage | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Under 15 years | Small leak, few damaged shingles | Repair. The roof has life left. |
| Under 15 years | Large leak, multiple problem areas | Get a professional inspection. May be a repair. |
| 15-20 years | Any leak | Start planning for replacement. Repair buys time. |
| 20+ years | Any | Replace. Asphalt shingle roofs in Kansas do not last past 25 years. |
| Any age | Storm damage (hail, wind) | File insurance claim. May qualify for full replacement. |

## Questions to Ask Your Roofer

1. **Are you licensed and insured in Kansas?** Get their license number and proof of insurance.
2. **Do you use subcontractors or your own crew?** You want the same crew that did the estimate.
3. **What manufacturer warranties do you offer?** Shingle warranties range from 25 years (basic) to lifetime (premium).
4. **What is your workmanship warranty?** Standard is 5-10 years on labor.
5. **Will you handle the insurance claim?** Good storm damage roofers will. Bad ones will ask you to sign over your check and disappear.
